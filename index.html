Ice Bath Recovery & Sauna Advice

Optimize your recovery with cold and heat therapy

üßä

Ice Bath Recovery

Cold water immersion for enhanced recovery and performance.

  • Optimal temperature: 10-15¬∞C
  • Duration: 10-15 minutes
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Improves circulation
  • Speeds muscle recovery
  • Boosts immune function

Safety Guidelines:

  • Start gradually - build tolerance over time
  • Never stay in longer than 15 minutes
  • Monitor breathing and heart rate
  • Warm up slowly after exiting
  • Consult doctor if you have heart conditions
üî•

Sauna Therapy

Heat therapy for relaxation, detoxification, and recovery.

  • Temperature: 80-100¬∞C
  • Session length: 10-20 minutes
  • Promotes relaxation
  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Enhances flexibility
  • Supports detoxification

Safety Guidelines:

  • Stay hydrated before and after
  • Start with shorter sessions
  • Listen to your body - exit if dizzy
  • Cool down gradually
  • Avoid alcohol before sauna

Sea Swimming Safety Tips

Stay safe while enjoying open water swimming

üåä

Before You Swim

  • Check weather and tide conditions
  • Know your exit points
  • Swim with a buddy when possible
  • Tell someone your plans
  • Check water temperature
  • Assess your swimming ability
‚ö†Ô∏è

Danger Signs to Watch For

  • Strong currents or rip tides
  • Large waves or rough conditions
  • Cold water shock risk
  • Jellyfish or marine life warnings
  • Poor visibility
  • Strong offshore winds
üèä

While Swimming

  • Stay within your depth
  • Don't fight against currents
  • Swim parallel to shore if caught in rip
  • Keep an eye on your entry point
  • Take regular breaks
  • Stay visible with bright swim cap
üÜò

Emergency Procedures

  • Float on your back if tired
  • Signal for help if in trouble
  • Don't panic - conserve energy
  • Call 999 for coastguard if needed
  • Know basic water rescue techniques
  • Always have a safety plan
